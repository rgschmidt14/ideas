How it Works (The "Gee-Wiz" Info)

This tool estimates pavement temperature based on a weighted average of the air temperature over the last 10 hours, giving more significance to recent temperatures. Pavement, especially dark asphalt, absorbs and retains a lot of heat from the sun.

Why the difference? A simple rule of thumb is that in direct sunlight, asphalt can be 40-60Â°F hotter than the air temperature. This tool uses a slightly more conservative estimate to be on the safe side.
