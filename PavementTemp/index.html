How it Works (The "Gee-Wiz" Info)

This tool estimates pavement temperature based on a weighted average of the air temperature over the last 10 hours, giving more significance to recent temperatures. Pavement, especially dark asphalt, absorbs and retains a lot of heat from the sun.

Why the difference? A simple rule of thumb is that in direct sunlight, asphalt can be 40-60°F hotter than the air temperature. This tool uses a slightly more conservative estimate to be on the safe side.

Safety Thresholds:

  • < 110°F (43°C): Generally safe for paws and feet.
  • 110°F - 130°F (43°C - 54°C): Caution advised. The pavement can be uncomfortable. Use the "7-second rule": if you can't comfortably hold the back of your hand on the surface for 7 seconds, it's too hot for paws.
  • > 130°F (54°C): DANGEROUS. Pavement at this temperature can cause burns in as little as 60 seconds. Avoid walking pets on pavement.

Sources: Four Paws USA, The Spruce Pets
